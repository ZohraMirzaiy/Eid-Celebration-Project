Eid Celebration at George Brown Afghan Student Association

A joyous gathering to celebrate Eid with Afghan culture, food, and community.

Event Details

Date:April First, 2024

From 18:00 to 20:00

George Brown College, Casa Loma Campus, Main Hall

To celebrate Eid, foster community spirit, and share Afghan traditions with George Brown College students and faculty.

Guests & Speakers

Freshta Hemmati

Afghan journalist finds new ways to fight against oppression and help other newcomers

Freshta Hemmati, an Afghan refugee journalist, fled to Canada in 2022 after the Taliban takeover threatened her work. Despite early challenges, including limited education as a refugee in Iran, she pursued journalism, earning a degree and working in Afghanistan. Forced to flee again in 2021, she completed a master's in Kazakhstan before arriving in Charlottetown. Now, she's a journalist, advocate for Afghan voices, and IRSA facilitator, expressing deep gratitude to Canada for its support.

Sheila Qayumi

Activist and teacher helping Afghan women settle in Canada

Sheila Qayumi, an Afghan women's rights advocate, fled Kabul in 2021. Despite leaving family behind, she continues her activism from Calgary. Formerly an English teacher and program manager for women's empowerment, she now works as an interpreter and counselor, while remaining a board member of Equality for Peace and Development. She actively supports Afghan women in Canada, helping them integrate and pursue education and employment.

Ahmad Siar Mateen

Afghan journalist finds new ways to fight against oppression and help other newcomers

Ahmad Siar Mateen, an Afghan comedian and refugee, used humor to navigate life under the Taliban and his move to Canada in 2022. Known for his uncensored voice and women's rights advocacy on "Laughter Night," he faced threats, forcing him into hiding after Kabul fell. Despite harrowing escapes and airport detentions, he found safety in Canada. Now, he's pursuing education and launching a YouTube comedy channel, expressing deep gratitude for Canada's support.
